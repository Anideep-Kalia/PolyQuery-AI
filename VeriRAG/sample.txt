What is a RAG system?
    A system that retrieves relevant documents and uses them to generate answers.
What is faithfulness in RAG?
    A measure of whether the answer is grounded in retrieved documents.
How does query expansion improve retrieval?
    It increases the chances of retrieving relevant documents.
What is the difference between dense and sparse retrieval?
    Dense uses embeddings (semantic), sparse uses keywords (exact match).
Why is faithfulness important in RAG systems?
    It ensures answers are based on retrieved context and not hallucinated.
How does retrieval quality affect grounded generation?
    Better retrieval leads to more accurate and grounded answers.
Why can irrelevant documents degrade answer quality?
    They introduce noise and confuse the model.

Does RAG eliminate hallucinations completely?
    No, it reduces but does not eliminate them.
Can RAG guarantee correct answers?
    No, it improves accuracy but cannot guarantee correctness.
Is faithfulness about generating creative answers?
    No, it is about grounding answers in retrieved data.
What should the system do if no relevant document is found?
    Respond that there is not enough information.
What happens if all retrieved documents are irrelevant?
    The answer quality degrades or should be declined.
How can we evaluate RAG performance?
    Using metrics like faithfulness, relevance, and correctness.
